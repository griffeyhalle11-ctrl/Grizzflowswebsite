### Stage Details

**1. INQUIRY (Speed is everything)**
- Respond within 5 minutes (this alone beats 80% of competitors)
- Book estimate within 24-48 hours
- Send confirmation text immediately

**2. ESTIMATE (Build trust in person)**
- Arrive on time or 5 min early
- Walk the garage with them, ask questions
- Point out things they might not have thought of
- Give range on the spot: "Based on what I'm seeing, we're looking at $X-Y"
- Book tentative date before leaving: "If you want to move forward, I have [date] open"

**3. QUOTE (Strike while hot)**
- Send written quote within 2 hours of estimate
- Use text for simple jobs, email for bigger jobs
- Include clear scope so there's no confusion

**4. BOOKING (Make it easy to say yes)**
- Collect 25% deposit to confirm (Venmo/Zelle)
- Send calendar invite
- Text reminder day before

**5. JOB (Over-deliver)**
- Text "On my way" when departing
- Before starting, walk through scope with customer
- Take before photos
- When done, walk through with customer, take after photos
- Collect payment before leaving

**6. REVIEW (Ask immediately)**
- Hand them phone with review link open
- Or text review request within 2 hours
- Follow up once if they don't leave one

### Objection Handling

#### "That's more than I expected" / "Too expensive"

```
"I totally get it - garage projects can definitely be an investment. Let me ask: 
is it the total that's the issue, or would it help to break this into phases? 

We could start with just the cleanout and junk removal for $[smaller amount], 
and then tackle the organization later when it makes sense for your budget."
```

**Alternative:**
```
"I hear you. Out of curiosity, what were you expecting it to be? 

[If reasonable gap] Let me see what I can do. If we skip [X], I could get it 
down to $[Y]. Would that work?"

[If wildly off] I appreciate the honesty. Unfortunately at that price point 
I'd be losing money. But tell you what - if you want to do some of the prep 
work yourself, like boxing up items you're keeping, I could take off $[X]."
```

## 4. Referral Program

### Program Design

**Offer: $50 Dual Incentive**
- Referrer gets: $50 credit toward next service OR $50 cash (their choice)
- New customer gets: $50 off their first job

**Why this works:**
- Cash option makes it real (not everyone needs another service)
- Dual-sided incentive gives referrer a reason to push
- $50 is meaningful but doesn't destroy margins on a $300-600 job
